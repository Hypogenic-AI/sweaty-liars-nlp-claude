# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (Deep-Read)

1. **[Prompt Leakage Effect and Defense Strategies for Multi-Turn LLM Interactions](2404.16251_Prompt_Leakage_Multi_Turn.pdf)**
   - Authors: Agarwal, Fabbri, Risher, Laban, Joty, Wu
   - Year: 2024 (EMNLP)
   - arXiv: 2404.16251
   - Why relevant: Directly tests effect of concealment instructions on leakage; shows secrecy instructions backfire on open-source models

2. **[Dataset and Lessons Learned from the 2024 SaTML LLM CTF Competition](2406.07954_SaTML_CTF_Competition.pdf)**
   - Authors: Debenedetti et al.
   - Year: 2024 (IEEE SaTML)
   - arXiv: 2406.07954
   - Why relevant: 137K adversarial chats; all defenses broken; decoy > concealment

3. **[Gandalf the Red: Adaptive Security for LLMs](2501.07927_Gandalf_Red.pdf)**
   - Authors: Pfister et al.
   - Year: 2025 (ICML)
   - arXiv: 2501.07927
   - Why relevant: 280K+ attack prompts from crowd-sourced red-teaming; defense levels provide natural comparison

4. **[Why Are My Prompts Leaked?](2408.02416_Why_Prompts_Leaked.pdf)**
   - Authors: Liang et al.
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2408.02416
   - Why relevant: Mechanistic explanation — attention heads copy prompts regardless of concealment instructions

5. **[Whispers in the Machine: Confidentiality in Agentic Systems](2402.06922_Whispers_Confidentiality.pdf)**
   - Authors: Evertz et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2402.06922
   - Why relevant: Secret-key game framework; tool integration amplifies leakage

6. **[Can LLMs Keep a Secret? (ConfAIde)](2310.17884_ConfAIde_Can_LLMs_Keep_Secret.pdf)**
   - Authors: Mireshghallah et al.
   - Year: 2024 (ICLR Spotlight)
   - arXiv: 2310.17884
   - Why relevant: Privacy benchmark with tiered evaluation; GPT-4 leaks private info 39% of time

7. **[Tricking LLM-Based NPCs into Spilling Secrets](2508.19288_Tricking_NPCs_Secrets.pdf)**
   - Authors: Shiomi et al.
   - Year: 2025 (ProvSec)
   - arXiv: 2508.19288
   - Why relevant: Game NPC secret-keeping; 10% attack success despite explicit prohibition
